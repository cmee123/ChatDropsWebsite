But how does AI consume water?

Artificial Intelligence models require huge data centers to operate, which house the computers necessary to handle large models. Data centers consume astronomical amounts of water to stay powered, cooled, and operational. One technology companyâ€™s self-owned data centers alone directly withdrew 29 billion liters and consumed (i.e., evaporated) more than 23 billion liters of freshwater for on-site cooling in 2023. These centers require freshwater, which is already a scarce resource and critical to our survival.

Generating the necessary electricity to power data centers is an extremely water-intensive process. The national average for hydroelectric water use is 2 gal (7.6 liters) of evaporated water per kWh of electricity consumed. Finally, water is critical for the manufacturing process of computer hardware. Discharged water often contains toxic chemicals and/or hazardous wastes, rendering it undrinkable for humans.

Increased use of AI is fueling a growing demand for large data centers and their capabilities, which increases global freshwater consumption.

Laws and Regulations

Since AI is a new technology and rapidly expanding, there is minimal environmental AI laws and regulations. The most well known recent act is the Artificial Intelligence Environmental Imapcts Act of 2024.

This act accomplishes the following:
- Requires the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) to carry out a comprehensive study on the environmental impacts of AI
- Recognizes that AI can be used for environmental benefits
- Mandates the establishment of a consortium of stakeholders to analyze both the positive and negative environmental impacts of AI
- Does not require companies to change their behavior or conduct an internal study on energy usage
